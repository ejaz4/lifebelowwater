Plastic Pollution

Over 17 million metric tons of plastic trash enter the ocean annually, posing a threat to marine life as well as contaminating the food supply. The figure is projected to triple by the year 2040 if no actions are taken.

Climate Change & Ocean Acidification

Ocean acidity has increased by 30% since the start of the Industrial Revolution, which complicates the ability of coral reefs and shellfish to survive. Rising sea temperatures also cause coral bleaching and loss of marine habitat.

Overfishing & Habitat Destruction

34% of the global stocks of fish are overexploited. Illegal fishing and destructive methods like bottom trawling are wiping out entire ecosystems.

üåç Global Initiatives to Protect Oceans

Governments and organizations worldwide are working together to safeguard marine biodiversity:

Recognizing the critical importance of healthy oceans, the United Nations established Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water. This goal aims to "conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development."

Efforts under this goal include reducing marine pollution, protecting marine ecosystems, minimizing ocean acidification, and regulating overfishing. Achieving these targets is essential for the health of our planet and future generations.

How You Can Help

    • Marine Protected Areas (MPAs): Over 16% of marine waters are conserved in order to prevent overfishing and habitat destruction.
    • Plastic Waste Reduction: The UK, Canada, and Kenya have outlawed single-use plastics.
    • Coral Reef Conservation: The Coral Triangle Initiative rehabilitates reefs in Asia-Pacific.
